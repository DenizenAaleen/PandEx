History of Development

The development of UNTBTBCS began in Java. At this time it was based on Pathfinder, due to being a class assignment requirement. It had fully implemented armor and weapons, as well as randomly generated enemies, and a level up system. Its character creation was limited to a name, picking very watered down classes and races. The classes and races only gave a stat bonus upon creation and upon level up. There were no unique skills or features, and they all played the same.
This was scrapped to create fully integrated races and classes, although they were still based on Pathfinder. The next time it was scrapped was when everyone began dropping support for Java, and the search for a new language began. Eventually GML was chosen, as this allowed for ease of access from various platforms. During this reboot period, the game began to take its own races and lore, and distanced itself from its Pathfinder origins, although it kept Pathfinder's classes.
GML had its own downsides, such as requiring complicated work arounds to, get input from the player for simple things such as name. Being able to do complicated things with ease but unable to do simple things without causing a headache slowed the development of the game, although its lore and systems continued growing outside of coding.
Currently development has aimed at making a simpler "proof of concept" game that is planned to run only in browers via javascript. After that is completed a 2D version of the game will be made with new features in GML, to aim at a larger audience, followed by a larger "offical" release with unique art and a full playable story, including both a campaign mode, and a sandbox mode that captures the feel of the first two implementations.
